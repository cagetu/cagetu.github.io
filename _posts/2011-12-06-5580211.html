Shouldn’t I gamma correct this texture as well? The answer is normally no, I should not. The reason for this is that a bump map does not contain light information, but height data. The purpose of the image is not to be displayed directly on screen, but rather to modulate the normal direction of a surface, and there is no need for such information to be gamma encoded. It is of course possible that the bump map has been gamma encoded anyway, and this can happen for several reasons, but in this particular case it is linear and require no correction.
(텍스쳐에 감마값이 encoding 된다!!! 즉, 텍스쳐 불러올 때 알 수 있다는 뜻인 듯...)

As for bump maps, think of it in terms of data for a second, not an image. Say you're displacing a surface 2 units using the max displace modifier. So if you want to displace a piece 1 unit, your map should have a value of 0.5 at that spot. But if you paint a value of 0.5 in your bitmap in photoshop, what you see on the screen may seem the wrong color, even though mathematically it is the correct value. 
So not all bumps are 1.0, it depends on how you created the image. If you specifically placed numerical values in your disp or bump map, then you need to keep the value at 1.0 to get the exact disp/bump you wanted. If you painted your bump map in photoshop until the image "looked correct" to you, then you may need to do a gamma shift. But that's somewhat arbitrary to begin with, since you are not directly looking at your disp/bump when you painted the map in photoshop. Which is why many people just fake it, and adjust the bump strength after the fact if it looks too shallow.
