게임에서는 가장 궁금한 세션인데, 아직 발표전인가 보네요. 소개를 보면...

The first part of the course includes speakers from the makers of several innovative game companies, such as Bungie, Intel, and Avalanche, as well as game-oriented research from Chalmers University of Technology. Topics covered in the first part include practical methods of real game-rendering pipelines and shipping game engines, order-independent transparency and novel GPU-friendly data structures, programmable blending, and clastered shading for lighting complex scenes in real-time.

 

The second part of the course includes speakers from the state-of-the-art games, such as Crytek, CD Project RED, and Studio Gobo. Topics covered in the second part of the course include practical methods of real game-rendering pipelines and shipping game engines, real-time radiosity solution targeted toward games, efficient rendering of ocean water, character pipeline, and novel methods for antialiasing, and variety of other graphics gems from shipping engines. We will complete the day with a panel amongst all of the speakers for parts I and II focused on practical game production pipelines and challenges for cross-generation development.


대략 저런 내용들을 발표할 것 같은데... 발표자료라도 빨리 보고 싶네요.. clastered shading 논문이 발표된지는 꽤 되었는데, 아직 사용했다는 내용을 본 적은 없어서, 이번에 사례를 보면 좀 더 이해가 될 것  같구요.

여러가지 기법들이 엔진에 적용한 사례들이 많이 소개될 것 같은데, 재밌는 내용이 많을 것 같네요.. 두근 두근!!!!

그래도 기법적으로 예전에 비해서 드라마틱하게 새로운 것들이 등장하지는 않는다는 느낌은데, 기술의 복잡도가 이미 엄청나게 증가한 것도 있고, 하드웨어의 발전속도도 약간은 영향을 주는 것이 아닌가 생각이 드네요..

더불어 "Siggraph2013 Course: Physically Based Shading in Theory and Practice"도 무척 궁금한데, 발표자료가 나오기를 기다려봐야겠네요..
